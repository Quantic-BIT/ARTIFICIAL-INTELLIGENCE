# Paid Time Off (PTO) Policy

## Overview

Acme Corporation provides paid time off to support work-life balance and employee well-being. This policy covers vacation, sick leave, and personal days.

## PTO Accrual

### Vacation Time

Vacation time accrues based on years of service:

| Years of Service | Annual Vacation Days | Monthly Accrual |
|-----------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| 0-2 years | 15 days | 1.25 days |
| 3-5 years | 20 days | 1.67 days |
| 6-10 years | 25 days | 2.08 days |
| 10+ years | 30 days | 2.50 days |

### Sick Leave

All full-time employees receive **10 sick days** per year. Sick leave:
- Accrues at 0.83 days per month
- Can be used for personal illness or to care for immediate family members
- Does not carry over to the next year
- Is not paid out upon termination

### Personal Days

All employees receive **3 personal days** per year for:
- Personal appointments
- Family events
- Mental health days
- Religious observances

## Requesting Time Off

### Vacation Requests

1. Submit requests through the HR portal at least **2 weeks in advance**
2. Requests are subject to manager approval
3. During peak business periods, requests may be limited
4. Seniority may be considered when multiple requests conflict

### Sick Leave Requests

1. Notify your manager as soon as possible, preferably before your shift starts
2. For absences of 3 or more consecutive days, a doctor's note may be required
3. Update your status in the HR portal

### Personal Day Requests

1. Submit requests at least **48 hours in advance** when possible
2. Manager approval is required
3. Emergency personal days should be reported as soon as possible

## Carryover and Maximum Balances

### Vacation Carryover
- Maximum carryover: **5 days** to the next calendar year
- Unused days beyond the limit will be forfeited on December 31st
- No payout for unused vacation except upon termination

### Sick Leave Carryover
- Sick leave does **not** carry over
- Unused sick days are forfeited at year end
- Sick leave is not paid out upon termination

## PTO Cash-Out

Employees may cash out up to **5 vacation days** per year:
- Request must be submitted by November 1st
- Payout will be included in December paycheck
- Only vacation days (not sick or personal) may be cashed out

## Blackout Periods

Certain periods may be designated as blackout periods where PTO is limited:
- Month-end close (Finance department)
- Product launches (Engineering and Marketing)
- Annual conferences and events

Managers will communicate blackout periods at least 30 days in advance.

## Holidays vs. PTO

Company holidays (see Holiday Schedule) are separate from PTO:
- Holidays do not count against your PTO balance
- If you must work on a holiday, you receive an alternative day off

## Part-Time Employees

Part-time employees receive prorated PTO based on their regular schedule:
- Example: An employee working 20 hours/week receives 50% of full-time PTO

## Extended Leave

For leaves exceeding available PTO:
- Unpaid leave may be requested with HR and manager approval
- FMLA leave may apply (see Benefits Policy for details)
- Extended sick leave may require short-term disability
