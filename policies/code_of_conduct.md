# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

This Code of Conduct establishes the standards of behavior expected from all Acme Corporation employees. It reflects our values and ensures a respectful, ethical, and productive workplace.

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees at every level
- Contractors and temporary workers
- Interns and volunteers
- Anyone representing Acme Corporation

## Core Principles

### Integrity
- Be honest in all business dealings
- Honor commitments and promises
- Report errors or mistakes promptly
- Do not misrepresent facts or data

### Respect
- Treat all individuals with dignity
- Value diverse perspectives and backgrounds
- Listen actively and communicate respectfully
- Recognize others' contributions

### Accountability
- Take responsibility for your actions
- Meet deadlines and commitments
- Admit mistakes and learn from them
- Follow policies and procedures

## Workplace Behavior

### Professional Conduct
Employees are expected to:
- Maintain professional appearance appropriate to role
- Be punctual for work and meetings
- Communicate respectfully in all channels
- Focus on work during business hours
- Represent the company positively

### Collaboration
- Share information and resources openly
- Support colleagues in achieving goals
- Provide constructive feedback
- Celebrate team successes

### Prohibited Behaviors
The following are strictly prohibited:
- Harassment of any kind
- Discrimination based on protected characteristics
- Bullying or intimidation
- Retaliation against reporters
- Violence or threats of violence
- Insubordination

## Anti-Harassment Policy

### Definition
Harassment includes unwelcome conduct based on:
- Race, color, or ethnicity
- Gender, gender identity, or sexual orientation
- Age
- Religion
- Disability
- National origin
- Any other protected characteristic

### Examples of Harassment
- Offensive jokes or slurs
- Physical intimidation or assault
- Unwanted sexual advances
- Displaying offensive materials
- Exclusion from activities based on protected characteristics

### Zero Tolerance
Acme Corporation has **zero tolerance** for harassment:
- All reports will be investigated promptly
- Confirmed harassment results in disciplinary action up to termination
- Retaliation against reporters is prohibited

## Anti-Discrimination Policy

We are committed to equal opportunity in:
- Hiring and recruitment
- Compensation and benefits
- Promotions and advancement
- Training and development
- All employment decisions

Discrimination based on race, gender, age, disability, religion, sexual orientation, or any protected class is prohibited and will result in disciplinary action.

## Conflicts of Interest

### Definition
A conflict of interest occurs when personal interests could influence business decisions.

### Examples
- Personal relationships with vendors or competitors
- Outside employment with competing businesses
- Financial interests in company decisions
- Hiring or supervising family members

### Disclosure
- Disclose potential conflicts to your manager and HR
- Recuse yourself from affected decisions
- Complete annual conflict of interest disclosure form
- When in doubt, disclose

## Gifts and Entertainment

### Receiving Gifts
- Gifts under $100 may be accepted
- Gifts over $100 must be reported to compliance
- Never accept cash or cash equivalents
- Decline gifts that could influence decisions

### Giving Gifts
- Business gifts must be reasonable and appropriate
- Follow expense policy guidelines
- Never give gifts to government officials without Compliance approval
- Document all business entertainment

## Confidentiality

### Protecting Information
- Keep confidential information private
- Do not discuss sensitive matters in public
- Secure documents and devices
- Follow data classification guidelines

### Post-Employment
Confidentiality obligations continue after employment:
- Do not take confidential information to new employers
- Honor non-disclosure agreements
- Return all company property upon departure

## Use of Company Resources

### Acceptable Use
Company resources (computers, email, internet) are for business use:
- Occasional personal use is acceptable if it doesn't interfere with work
- Never use for illegal activities
- Never use for harassment or discrimination
- Never use for personal business ventures

### Monitoring
Company reserves the right to monitor:
- Email communications
- Internet usage
- Computer files
- Company devices

Employees should not expect privacy when using company resources.

## Social Media

### Personal Social Media
- Clearly distinguish personal views from company positions
- Do not share confidential company information
- Be respectful of colleagues and the company
- Avoid engaging in disputes about the company online

### Company Social Media
- Only authorized employees may post on company channels
- Follow brand guidelines
- Get approval for campaigns and announcements
- Report inappropriate content to Marketing

## Reporting Violations

### How to Report
If you witness or experience a violation:
1. **Direct Manager**: For most workplace issues
2. **Human Resources**: hr@acmecorp.com
3. **Ethics Hotline**: 1-800-ACME-ETH (anonymous)
4. **Compliance Portal**: ethics.acmecorp.com

### Non-Retaliation
- All reports are taken seriously
- Reporters are protected from retaliation
- Anonymous reporting is available
- False reports made in bad faith may result in discipline

## Enforcement

### Investigation Process
All reports are:
- Investigated promptly and fairly
- Kept confidential to the extent possible
- Resolved with appropriate actions

### Disciplinary Actions
Violations may result in:
- Verbal warning
- Written warning
- Suspension
- Demotion
- Termination
- Legal action

Severity of action depends on nature and frequency of violation.

## Acknowledgment

All employees must:
- Read and understand this Code of Conduct
- Sign acknowledgment upon hire
- Complete annual ethics training
- Re-acknowledge annually
